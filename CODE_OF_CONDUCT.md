# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Lint Sage is built for repeatable, low-friction collaboration. We want this repository to be a respectful, welcoming place for maintainers, contributors, and first-time participants alike.

By participating in this project, you agree to help create an environment where people can ask questions, share ideas, and give technical feedback without harassment or hostility.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and constructive in issues, pull requests, code review, and documentation discussions.
- Focus feedback on the work, not the person.
- Assume good intent, especially when someone is new to the codebase or to open source contribution.
- Share enough context for others to understand technical decisions, tradeoffs, and risks.
- Welcome differing viewpoints and experience levels.
- Respect privacy and security. Do not share private information, credentials, or exploit details in public threads.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable in this project:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or personal attacks
- Discriminatory or demeaning language related to identity, background, or experience level
- Trolling, baiting, dogpiling, or deliberately derailing technical discussion
- Publishing private information without permission
- Sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention
- Repeatedly dismissing contributors in bad faith or refusing to engage respectfully after moderation

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for setting the tone of collaboration and protecting the health of the project. They may:

- Clarify expected behavior
- Moderate, edit, or remove comments that violate this policy
- Close discussions that are abusive, off-topic, or unproductive
- Temporarily or permanently restrict participation when needed to protect the community

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, reviews, and discussions
- Documentation and design conversations
- Any external space where someone is representing this project or its maintainers

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, contact the maintainers privately through a non-public channel when possible. If you are a ViitorCloud team member, use the internal communication path you normally use for sensitive concerns.

If you do not already have a private contact route, post a brief public note asking a maintainer to provide one, without including sensitive personal details in the public thread.

When making a report, include any context that can help the maintainers investigate, such as links, screenshots, timestamps, or a short summary of what happened. Reports will be reviewed as confidentially as practical.

If the concern involves a specific maintainer, please contact another maintainer or an appropriate ViitorCloud manager instead of reporting only to the person involved.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they believe is appropriate, including:

- A private warning
- Removal of comments or contributions
- Temporary participation limits
- Permanent removal from project spaces
